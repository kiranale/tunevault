## SUMMARY

ACME-PROD is a busy Oracle 19c EBS R12.2.10 production instance running on 16-core Linux with 24 GB SGA. Overall health score is 67/100, which reflects functional operation but with three compounding risks that require resolution within the next 48 hours.

The most urgent issue is the USERS tablespace at 92.5% utilization with only 247 MB of free space. At the current ingestion rate of approximately 120 MB/day, the tablespace will become full within 2–3 days. An ORA-01653 error at that point will halt all INSERT activity across the EBS application, affecting order entry, AR processing, and the Concurrent Manager. This is straightforward to fix with a single ALTER TABLESPACE statement.

RMAN full backups have not completed in 9 days — two scheduled backup jobs were skipped on May 5 and May 8. Archivelog backups are current (14 hours old), which limits the immediate data-loss exposure, but without a recent full backup the recovery time objective (RTO) becomes unacceptably long. Recovery from the last full backup would require replaying 9 days of archived logs, which in a busy EBS environment could take 6–10 hours.

## TOP_RISKS

1. USERS tablespace exhaustion — 2–3 days to ORA-01653. Add datafile immediately.

2. RMAN backup gap — 9 days without a full backup, RPO unacceptable for production EBS. Re-run full backup tonight.

3. Default passwords on 4 accounts — DBSNMP, SCOTT, HR, OUTLN remain accessible with vendor-default credentials. Lock or rotate before end of day.

4. SQL bq9vzn5r1kf22 — 18.2M buffer gets per execution with 892 executions today. Missing composite index on OE_ORDER_LINES_ALL(CUSTOMER_ID, ORDER_DATE) is the root cause. Index creation runs online with no downtime.

5. ADOP cleanup pending — 8 days since patch completion. Run cleanup to release disk and enable future patch cycles.
